Building Reliable Infrastructure: The Redbeard Automation Pipeline

Published on March 19, 2026 by Redbeard Solutions

At Redbeard Solutions, we don’t just build code; we build the pipelines that make that code reliable. Over the past few weeks, our focus has shifted from rapid, project-based prototyping to tightening up our internal operational pipelines. We’ve been refining how we develop, deploy, and maintain our own infrastructure to ensure our client solutions are as resilient as they are innovative.

Continuous Deployment Workflows

A major focus has been streamlining our deployment workflow. We’ve integrated our local AI-assisted development workspace directly with our GitHub repository, ensuring that every iteration on our site and projects goes through a version-controlled pipeline. This shift to GitHub Pages as our hosting platform means we can move fast, test changes locally, and deploy with confidence, knowing our history is secure and our environment is reproducible.

The Power of AI-Assisted Development

This entire site—and the internal tooling that manages it—is an example of our "AI-first" development philosophy. We use AI agents not just to write code, but to track our time, manage project site updates, and iterate on complex UI designs. By offloading the heavy lifting of project documentation and version management to our own agentic workflows, we can dedicate more energy to solving the core business challenges our clients face.

Persistent Infrastructure: From Script to Service

We’ve been moving away from "it works on my machine" toward a "production-grade" standard for everything we build. Whether it’s maintaining our Time Tracker web application, managing local node automation, or engineering complex smart environment integrations, we’ve prioritized creating stable, reboot-resilient service environments. Reliability is the silent hallmark of quality, and our recent work focuses on ensuring that when we build a solution, it stays built.

Proving Capability: Identity Modernization

One of our foundational capabilities is our experience in identity management and zero-touch SSO, which we’ve leveraged in past projects to manage large-scale device fleets. This experience—automating user lifecycles, integrating JAMF with Azure AD, and streamlining provisioning—continues to inform how we build our current projects. By viewing identity as programmable infrastructure, we’ve learned how to build systems that scale effortlessly, regardless of the size of the team or fleet.

As we continue to iterate on our automation pipelines, our goal remains clear: to build software and systems that don’t just function, but persist. We’re eager to bring this same level of rigor and reliability to your next project.
